Welcome to Nicholas Wulf’s game development site!

 

This is my first game with OpenGL!  It’s fairly simple but still kind of fun.  The goal of the game is to hit the other player’s cannon with a well placed shot.  Use A and D to control the angle and W and S to change the power of your shot.  When you’re all ready to fire then just press the space bar and watch your bullet fly across the map and hopefully hit your opponent.  You and your opponent will continue to take turns firing at each other until one of you decides to quit (since there is no score limit).  Once one of you is hit, a new level will generate and you’ll have to figure out the terrain all over again.

 

I guess I’ll call this game Cannon.  Anyway, right below this is a link for downloading the C++ files for the game followed by some screen shots.  This game was made for Assignment 1 of my CAP 4730 class so the bottom of this page is used to address some of the discussion topics that are required for this assignment.  Whatever… Enjoy!

Discussion Questions:

 

1)    Explain the visual result of drawing two overlapping objects

 

If two objects are drawn to the same place on the screen, then one object will overlap the other depending on the state of GL_DEPTH_TEST, the z-coordinates of the objects, and the order in which they are drawn to the screen.  If GL_DEPTH_TEST is disabled then the z-coordinates of the drawings do not affect the order of overlapping.  Instead, it is the object that is last drawn to the screen that will overlap the others.  In this way having GL_DEPTH_TEST disabled simulates a real life canvas in that objects that are drawn are overlapped by objects that are drawn afterwards.  Enabling GL_DEPTH_TEST simulates more of a 3D environment.  In this mode objects are ordered on screen depending on their z-coordinates with a higher z value indicating a farther distance away from the screen.  Thus, objects with a higher z value will be overlapped by objects with a lower z value, which is considered to be closer to the screen.  If two objects have the same z value, the object that is drawn first will overlap the object that is drawn afterward.  Therefore, for the special case that all z values are equal, enabling GL_DEPTH_TEST will create an order of overlapping that is the complete opposite of disabling GL_DEPTH_TEST.

 

2)    Make a graph of number of objects drawn (~10, ~100, ~1000, ~10000, ~100000 objects per frame vs. the frames per second)

 

 

This chart shows the kind of proportional inverse relationship that we would expect from this kind of test.  Obviously a computer with a finite amount of processing power will take twice as long to perform twice as many operations.  Of special note are the flat zones at 60 and 30 FPS.  This is caused by the fact that the glutSwapBuffers() function tries to synch itself with multiples of a 60th of a second.  We should also see flat zones around 20, 15, 12, and 10 FPS, however the resolution along the x-axis is not fine enough to detect these occurrences.
